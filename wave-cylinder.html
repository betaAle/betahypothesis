A wave propagating on the D¹ × S¹ bundle

Rest observer watching a massive particle. The total speed along the helix is always c; only its partition between the observable base (vₓ) and the internal fiber (v₀) changes.

At rest (vₓ = 0) the wave is pure internal rotation — the wavefronts are rings turning in place, and the internal clock ticks at its maximum rate. As vₓ increases, part of the motion is diverted along x: the helix stretches, and the internal rotation (hence the particle's own clock) slows as v₀ = c/γ. As vₓ → c the helix straightens toward the axis and the internal clock freezes.
