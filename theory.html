Geometric foundations

Theory

A local geometric constraint on a fiber bundle, from which relativistic kinematics is reconstructed rather than postulated.

The base structure: D¹ × S¹

The starting point is a fiber bundle whose base is the observable direction of motion (D¹) and whose fiber is an internal circular degree of freedom (S¹). Each point carries both an observable position and an internal phase. Rest mass is identified with internal winding on S¹: a particle at rest is pure phase rotation, with no motion along the base.

The local constraint

Instead of postulating Lorentz transformations, a local relation links the rate of change of the base coordinate to the rate of change of the internal phase. This constraint is geometric, not dynamical: it holds pointwise, independently of any global symmetry assumption.

Energy and mass: one vibration, two readings

Rest energy and relativistic energy are not separate quantities connected by a formula — they are the same internal vibration, measured as a wavefront rate in two different frames. The ontological content m₀c² is invariant; the observational content γm₀c² is what governs interactions.

Time as an internal clock

Every massive particle carries its own proper time, given by the period of its own internal rotation on S¹. There is no privileged external clock: dilation between observers is relational, recovered from the local constraint rather than assumed.

Lorentz invariance as a consequence

Lorentz invariance is not assumed. It emerges as a consistency structure: the set of transformations that preserve the local constraint coincides with the Lorentz group on D¹ × S¹. This reframes special relativity as a derived feature of the bundle geometry rather than an external postulate.

The Gudermannian bridge

The relation between the circular geometry of the internal phase and the hyperbolic geometry of rapidity is given by the Gudermannian function. It provides the explicit bridge connecting the compact S¹ structure to the non-compact boost structure of relativistic kinematics.

The dispersion cone

The local constraint, expressed in terms of observable and internal frequencies, traces out a cone in frequency space. This dispersion cone is the geometric image of the relativistic energy-momentum relation, recovered here as a feature of the bundle rather than introduced by hand.

Extension to D³ × S³: spin and charge

Generalizing the fiber from S¹ to S³ ≅ SU(2) is required to support half-integer representations, which S¹ cannot carry. This extension is the subject of ongoing work (Beta 2), where spin and electric charge are identified with the SU(2)L and SU(2)R sectors of the internal rotation group.
