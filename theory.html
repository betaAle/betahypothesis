Geometric foundations

Theory

A local geometric constraint on a fiber bundle, from which relativistic kinematics is reconstructed rather than postulated.

The base structure: D¹ × S¹

The starting point is a fiber bundle whose base is the observable direction of motion (D¹) and whose fiber is an internal circular degree of freedom (S¹). Each point carries both an observable position and an internal phase. Rest mass is identified with internal winding on S¹: a particle at rest is pure phase rotation, with no motion along the base.

The local constraint

Instead of postulating Lorentz transformations, a local relation links the rate of change of the base coordinate to the rate of change of the internal phase. This constraint is geometric, not dynamical: it holds pointwise, independently of any global symmetry assumption.

Lorentz invariance as a consequence

Lorentz invariance is not assumed. It emerges as a consistency structure: the set of transformations that preserve the local constraint coincides with the Lorentz group on D¹ × S¹. This reframes special relativity as a derived feature of the bundle geometry rather than an external postulate.
