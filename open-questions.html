Beta Hypothesis

A geometric framework for relativistic dynamics

Can relativistic phenomena emerge from a local geometric constraint acting on an internal circular degree of freedom?

The central local constraint is

\[v_x^2 + v_\theta^2 = c^2\]

Beta Hypothesis is an ongoing speculative framework exploring whether time dilation, relativistic energy, and wave-particle relations can be interpreted as consequences of motion on a fiber bundle \(D^1 \times S^1\).

Status: Beta Hypothesis is a research proposal under development. The website distinguishes derived relations, working assumptions, and open problems. It is not presented as an experimentally established replacement for standard relativity.

Geometry

A particle is represented through observable motion in \(D^1\) and an internal cyclic degree of freedom \(S^1\), constrained by a fixed total phase velocity.
