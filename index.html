β Beta Hypothesis

The Beta Hypothesis explores the possibility that relativistic phenomena such as time dilation, length contraction and relativistic energy may emerge from a geometric constraint linking observable motion and internal periodic dynamics.

The basic structure is a fiber bundle of the form D¹ × S¹, where motion along the observable direction and motion along an internal circular degree of freedom are constrained by a local relation.

Why Beta?

Instead of taking Lorentz transformations as the starting point, the Beta Hypothesis begins with a local geometric constraint. The aim is to investigate whether the familiar effects of relativistic kinematics can be reconstructed as consequences of this underlying geometry.

Three Key Ideas

Geometric Constraint

Particles are described through a geometric structure in which observable motion and internal phase motion are locally connected.

Harmonic Energy

Rest energy and relativistic energy are interpreted as different manifestations of an underlying harmonic structure.

Open Research

The present work focuses mainly on relativistic kinematics. Extensions toward electromagnetism, gravitation and quantum theory remain open research directions.
