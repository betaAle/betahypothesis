SR vs Beta: Logical Sequencing

Both theories arrive at the same physics — Lorentz transformations, the Minkowski metric, the mass shell. What differs is the order in which these appear, and what has to be assumed to get them. Special relativity postulates two physical axioms and derives the geometry. Beta postulates a single local geometric constraint and derives the rest — including, in later work, spin and charge.

The reversal, at a glance

The same three landmarks appear in both chains, but their positions are inverted:

Dispersion cone — first derived object in Beta (step 5), but the final derived link in SR (step 5, last).
Lorentz transformations — near-primitive in SR (derived immediately from the two postulates), but a late consequence in Beta (step 10).
Minkowski metric — the primitive arena in SR's usual reading; in Beta, the emergent geometry of consistency among observers (step 11).
Rest mass — an external input in SR; in Beta, derived from the geometry of the internal fiber (step 6).

The conceptual hinge

SR postulates two physical axioms — relativity and the invariance of c — and reads the geometry off them. Beta postulates a single geometric object — a flat bundle with a local constraint — and derives both the kinematic structure (dispersion, Lorentz, Minkowski) and, in later work, the internal quantum numbers (spin, charge).

One foundational act generates more structure, from fewer independent assumptions — at the cost of a less immediately intuitive substrate, and of a longer derivation chain. In SR the dispersion cone is where the reasoning ends; in Beta it is where it begins.
