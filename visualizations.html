Core framework

The Beta Hypothesis

A speculative geometric approach in which relativistic behavior is investigated as an emergent consequence of a local constraint on a fiber bundle.

1. Geometric setting

The basic configuration space is written as \(D^1 \times S^1\), where \(D^1\) denotes the observable direction of propagation and \(S^1\) denotes an internal circular degree of freedom. The internal coordinate is not treated as an ordinary hidden spatial dimension, but as a cyclic phase-like structure.

The central intuition is that a particle always preserves a fixed total local phase velocity, distributed between observable propagation and internal cyclic evolution.

\[v_x^2 + v_\theta^2 = c^2\]

2. Relativistic dynamics

In the Beta picture, rest mass is associated with an internal period \(T_0\) and a Compton-like length scale \(\lambda_0\):

\[\lambda_0 = \frac{h}{m_0 c}, \qquad T_0 = \frac{\lambda_0}{c}=\frac{h}{m_0 c^2}.\]

The framework then explores whether the usual relativistic energy growth can be reconstructed from the geometry of the internal and observable components.

\[E^2=(m_0c^2)^2\left[\left(\frac{\lambda_x}{\lambda_0}\right)^2+1\right]\]

3. Wave formalism

The wave representation is expressed through a phase field of the form

\[\psi(x,\theta,t)=A e^{i(k_x x+k_\theta\theta-\omega t)}.\]

Phase invariance is treated as a guiding principle. The framework aims to recover observable relativistic relations without taking coordinate transformations as the starting axiom.

\[k^2=k_x^2+k_\theta^2, \qquad \omega = ck\]

4. Interpretation

Beta is best read as an attempt to reinterpret familiar relativistic effects through a deeper geometric mechanism. Its value depends on mathematical consistency, recovery of known tests, and the possibility of identifying clear experimental or conceptual distinctions from standard Special Relativity.
